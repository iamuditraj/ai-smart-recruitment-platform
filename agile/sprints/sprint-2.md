# 🧠 Sprint 2 – Literature Review & Research

## 1️⃣ Sprint Goal

The goal of Sprint 2 was to conduct an in-depth literature review and domain research on AI-based recruitment systems, resume screening using Natural Language Processing (NLP), machine learning-based candidate ranking, and Agile software development methodology.

This sprint focused on understanding existing research, identifying gaps, and defining a strong technical foundation for implementation in Phase 1.

---

## 2️⃣ Problem Context

Traditional recruitment systems face multiple limitations:

- Manual resume screening is time-consuming and inefficient.
- Keyword-based ATS systems fail to evaluate contextual skills.
- Skill assessment is often conducted late in the hiring process.
- Human bias affects shortlisting decisions.
- Lack of explainability in AI-based hiring systems.

This research phase was conducted to design a more intelligent, scalable, and unbiased recruitment solution.

---

## 3️⃣ Research Areas Explored

### 🔹 A. AI in Recruitment

Research focused on how Artificial Intelligence is transforming hiring processes.

Key Insights:
- AI automates resume parsing and candidate matching.
- Predictive analytics improves hiring accuracy.
- AI-based screening reduces human workload.
- Ethical AI and bias mitigation are critical concerns.

Learning Outcome:
AI can significantly improve recruitment efficiency, but fairness and transparency must be integrated into system design.

---

### 🔹 B. Resume Screening using NLP

Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques were studied for extracting meaningful information from resumes.

Technologies Explored:
- Named Entity Recognition (NER)
- Keyword Extraction
- Text Similarity Matching
- Skill Extraction using spaCy

Approach Identified:
- Parse resume text
- Extract skills, education, and experience
- Match against job description
- Generate skill relevance score

Learning Outcome:
NLP-based contextual understanding performs better than traditional keyword matching systems.

---

### 🔹 C. Candidate Ranking using Machine Learning

Research focused on ranking candidates based on weighted scoring mechanisms.

Techniques Studied:
- TF-IDF similarity
- Cosine similarity
- Weighted scoring algorithms
- Feature-based ranking systems

Proposed Method:
Each candidate will receive a weighted score based on:
- Skill match
- Experience relevance
- Assessment performance
- Interview performance

Learning Outcome:
Combining multiple evaluation metrics provides a more balanced and accurate ranking model.

---

### 🔹 D. Agile & Scrum Methodology

The Scrum framework was analyzed to structure project execution.

Key Scrum Concepts Applied:
- Sprint Planning
- Product Backlog
- Incremental Delivery
- Sprint Review & Retrospective

Reason for Choosing Agile:
- Allows modular development
- Encourages iterative improvement
- Supports collaborative academic projects
- Easy to track progress through GitHub Issues

Learning Outcome:
Agile methodology ensures structured and manageable project development.
